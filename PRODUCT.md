# Product: Guardians of Humanity Gamification

## 1. Overview

**Concept:** A hybrid "Game-Lesson" (both in-person and online) designed as an interactive and engaging learning experience. It leverages the Guy Kawasaki presentation methodology (10 slides) to deliver concise, visual, and impactful educational content.

**Project Focus:** To create interactive educational material for the "Agent Training" course.

**Target Audience:** Students enrolled in the "Agent Training" course.

**Theoretical Foundation:** The curriculum is based on the "Agent Specialization Program," incorporating themes such as the Invisible College and the Coherence between Discourse and Practice.

## 2. System Components

The system is composed of two main elements:

### a. Dynamic Presentation
A slide-based presentation following the dynamic and visual Kawasaki structure.

### b. Physical/Digital Cards
Pocket-sized cards (6x9 cm) that are central to the experience. Each card represents one of the 12 unique entities/characters (e.g., Guardian Anton, Father John, Guardian Astrid) and serves as a key to unlock specific content and interactions.

## 3. Card Structure

Each of the 12 character cards will feature:

- **Design:** A striking and memorable visual identity that includes symbology related to the entity.
- **Attributes:** Concise information connecting the character to the corresponding lesson's theme.
- **Mechanics:** An interactive action or a challenge that the student must perform to "activate" the card and its content.

## 4. Gamification Dynamics

The learning process is structured as a narrative-driven training mission for new agents.

- **Engaging Narrative:** The learning journey progresses as a mission to train agents, making the experience more immersive.
- **Game Mechanics:**
    - **Unlockables:** Activating cards unlocks new content, in-lesson bonuses, or deeper lore.
    - **Challenges:** Students solve real-world problems presented in the course by applying the characteristics and knowledge of the Guardians.
    - **Progression:** A system of symbolic rewards and a scoring mechanism is implemented to encourage collaboration, healthy competition, and continuous engagement.
